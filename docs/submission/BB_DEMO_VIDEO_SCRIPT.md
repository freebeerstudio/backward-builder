## HOOK — The Problem (0:00 – 0:35)

> Speak directly to camera or over your screen. Confident, conversational.

**Say something like:**

"Every teacher in America is trained in backward design — start with what students need to understand, then build assessments and instruction backward from there. It's the gold standard. It's called Understanding by Design, and it's in every education program in the country.

But here's the problem: the tools teachers actually have are blank Word documents, generic templates, and maybe 45 minutes of planning time that's already eaten up by grading and emails. Teachers know exactly what great curriculum looks like — they just don't have a way to build it digitally.

I know this because I spent years as a middle school teacher, then moved into edtech, school administration, and eventually enterprise technology. I've seen this gap from every angle. Backward Builder is the tool I wished I'd had in the classroom."

> ~35 seconds. Adjust naturally — don't read verbatim. Hit the key beats:
> 1. Teachers know backward design
> 2. Their tools are garbage
> 3. You lived this problem personally

## LIVE DEMO — The Core Loop (0:35 – 3:15)

> This is the most important section. Show the app working end-to-end.
> Narrate WHY you're doing things, not just WHAT you're clicking.

### Step 1: Enter the Enduring Understanding (~30 sec)

**Say something like:**

"So let's say I'm a 7th grade science teacher planning a unit on ecosystems. I start where UbD says to start — with the enduring understanding. I'll type: 'Students will understand that organisms in an ecosystem are interdependent, and changes to one population affect the entire system.'"

> Type it live. Don't paste from clipboard — judges notice.

"The AI takes that understanding and helps me refine it if needed — but the key insight here is that the TEACHER is the curriculum expert. The AI doesn't decide what students should learn. The teacher does. The AI just helps build it."

### Step 2: Assessment Generation (~45 sec)

**Say something like:**

"Now Backward Builder moves to Stage 2 of UbD — the assessment. What evidence would prove a student actually understands ecosystem interdependence?"

> Show the AI generating assessment options.

"It's giving me several assessment options aligned to my enduring understanding — [describe what you see: maybe a performance task, a written explanation, a scenario-based assessment]. I can select the ones that fit my classroom, modify them, or combine them. This is where teacher judgment meets AI speed."

> Select an assessment option. Click through it deliberately.

"I'm going with [your choice] because [brief reason — e.g., 'it tests application, not just recall, which is what UbD is really about']."

### Step 3: Live Quiz Deployment (~45 sec)

**Say something like:**

"Here's where it gets exciting. Backward Builder takes that assessment and generates a deployable quiz — right now, ready for students."

> Show the quiz being generated. Click into it.

"This isn't a PDF I have to print. It's a live, shareable quiz. Students hit this link, take the assessment, and their responses are auto-graded. Let me show you what that looks like from a student's perspective."

> Open the student-facing quiz. Answer a couple questions live to show it works.

"And on the teacher side — here's the results dashboard. I can see responses coming in, see which questions students struggled with, and adjust my instruction before the next class. No manual grading. No spreadsheets."

> Show the dashboard. If possible, show a result appearing from what you just submitted.

### Step 4: Learning Activities (~30 sec)

**Say something like:**

"Finally — Stage 3 of UbD. Now that I know what I'm assessing, what learning activities get students there? Backward Builder generates a sequenced set of activities, all aligned backward from the assessment."

> Show the generated activities.

"Each activity builds toward the enduring understanding. The sequence matters — and the AI designs it so each step scaffolds the next. A teacher can take this plan and start teaching tomorrow."

## TECHNICAL DEPTH (Optional but Powerful) (3:15 – 3:50)

> Pick ONE technically interesting thing. 35 seconds max.

**Options — choose whichever matches what you actually built:**

**Option A: AI Prompt Architecture**
"Under the hood, this isn't a single API call. The AI flow is multi-step — first it analyzes the enduring understanding against Bloom's taxonomy to determine the cognitive level, then it generates assessments calibrated to that level, then it reverse-engineers learning activities. Each step feeds context to the next."

**Option B: Quiz Deployment**
"The quiz engine generates a unique shareable link for each assessment. Student responses hit the backend in real time, get auto-scored, and update the teacher dashboard with no page refresh. The teacher doesn't configure anything — they just share the link."

**Option C: Alignment Engine**
"The hardest part of this build wasn't generating content — it was keeping everything aligned. The AI uses the enduring understanding as an anchor throughout. Every assessment question maps back to it, and every learning activity maps to the assessment. If the chain breaks, it's not UbD anymore."

## RECORDING TIPS

- **One take is fine.** Don't over-edit. Authenticity scores better than polish.
- **Type live.** Don't paste from clipboard. Enter the enduring understanding by typing it in real time.
- **Show a real flow.** Start from a blank state, not a pre-loaded demo.
- **If something breaks:** Say "let me try that again" — it shows confidence and proves it's real.
- **Audio matters more than video.** Use a headset or sit close to a mic.
- **Add captions.** Judges may skim on mute first. CapCut auto-generates them for free.
- **File format:** MP4, under 100MB, under 5 minutes.

---

## WORDS TO USE / WORDS TO AVOID

### Use These
- "enduring understanding" (shows you know UbD)
- "aligned" / "alignment" (the core value proposition)
- "teacher judgment" / "professional expertise" (positions teacher as expert)
- "deploy" / "deployable" (shows it's not just a plan — it's live software)
- "no developer required" (ties directly to Challenge #2)

### Avoid These
- "AI-powered" without showing what that means
- "We used the Claude API" without explaining HOW
- "This is like ChatGPT for teachers" (undersells the product)
- "We didn't have time to..." (reframe as "what we'd build next")
