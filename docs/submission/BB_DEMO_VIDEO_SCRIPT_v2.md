## SECTION 1: THE STORY — Why This Exists (0:00 - 0:45)

> Camera or voiceover. Confident, personal, conversational. This is YOUR story.

**Say something like:**

"When I was a middle school teacher, I knew exactly what great teaching looked like. I'd been trained in Understanding by Design — start with what students need to understand, then design assessments backward from there, then build instruction that gets students ready. It's the gold standard. Every education program in the country teaches it.

But here's what they don't tell you: the tools teachers actually have are Word documents and blank templates. And that 45 minutes of planning time? It's gone before you sit down — eaten by grading, emails, and parent calls.

I spent my planning periods wishing I could just SAY what my students needed to understand and have something build the rest. But that tool didn't exist.

I went on to earn a doctorate in education, work in edtech and school administration, and eventually move into enterprise technology. I've seen this gap from every angle — the teacher side, the admin side, and the tech side. And now, with AI, I finally built what I wished I'd had in that classroom."

> ~45 seconds. Key beats:
> 1. Personal credibility — YOU were a teacher (not hypothetical)
> 2. Ed.D. — academic credibility in one sentence
> 3. The pain is real and specific (45 min planning, Word docs, no tools)
> 4. The gap: domain expert locked out of building (ties to Stacey/Vibe the Gap)

## SECTION 2: THE DEMO — Showing the Core Loop (0:45 - 3:15)

> This is 80% of the video. Show the app working end-to-end.
> Narrate WHY, not just WHAT. Type live — never paste.

### Step 1: Start with the Enduring Understanding (~30 sec)

**Say something like:**

"Here's what a teacher's experience looks like. I'm a 7th grade science teacher. I open Backward Builder and I start where UbD says to start — with the enduring understanding."

> Type live: "Students will understand that organisms in an ecosystem are interdependent, and changes to one population affect the entire system."

"That's it. That's all the teacher needs to provide — their domain expertise. What students need to understand. The AI handles the rest, but the teacher drives the curriculum. This isn't ChatGPT generating a generic lesson plan. This is a structured UbD pipeline that keeps everything aligned."

### Step 2: Stage 1 — Standards and Bloom's (~30 sec)

**Say something like:**

"The AI takes that understanding and does something a teacher would spend 20 minutes doing manually — it maps to my state standards and classifies the cognitive level using Bloom's Taxonomy."

> Show the standards alignment and Bloom's classification.

"This matters because alignment is what makes UbD work. If your assessment doesn't match your standard, if your activities don't match your assessment — you've broken the chain. Backward Builder maintains that chain automatically."

### Step 3: Stage 2 — Performance Tasks and Checks (~45 sec)

**Say something like:**

"Now Stage 2 — the evidence. What would prove a student actually understands ecosystem interdependence? The AI generates a GRASPS performance task — that's Goal, Role, Audience, Situation, Product, Standards — with a detailed rubric."

> Show the performance task and rubric.

"And here's where it gets powerful for daily classroom use — it also generates Checks for Understanding with auto-graded questions. Not a quiz I have to grade by hand. A live, deployable check that students can take right now."

> Click into a Check for Understanding. Show the share link.

"I share this link with my class. Students open it on any device — phone, Chromebook, tablet. No login, no app download. They take the check, it's graded instantly, and I see results in real time on my dashboard."

> Show a student taking the check (open the share link, answer a couple questions).

"On the teacher side — here's what I see. Per-question analytics. I can see exactly which concepts my students understand and which ones need reteaching. No spreadsheets. No manual grading. This is data I can act on before the next class."

> Show the results dashboard.

### Step 4: Stage 3 — Learning Plan (~30 sec)

**Say something like:**

"Finally — Stage 3. Now that I know what I'm assessing, what learning activities get students there? The AI generates a sequenced learning plan where every activity scaffolds toward the performance task."

> Show the learning activities.

"Each activity builds on the last. The sequence isn't random — it's designed backward from the assessment, exactly the way UbD works. A teacher can take this plan and start teaching tomorrow."

### Step 5: Share and Collaborate (~20 sec)

**Say something like:**

"And because great curriculum shouldn't live in one teacher's laptop — I can share this unit directly with a colleague by email, or publish it to the community library where any teacher can discover it."

> Show the share modal and community publish toggle briefly.

"Teachers sharing their best work with each other. That's how the whole profession gets better."

## SECTION 3: TECHNICAL DEPTH — What's Under the Hood (3:15 - 3:45)

> Pick ONE. 30 seconds. Show sophistication.

**Recommended: The AI Pipeline**

"Under the hood, this isn't a single API call. It's a multi-step pipeline. First, the AI analyzes the enduring understanding against Bloom's Taxonomy to determine the cognitive level. Then it generates performance tasks calibrated to that level. Then it creates checks aligned to the rubric criteria. Then it reverse-engineers learning activities. Each step feeds context to the next — and the enduring understanding anchors every downstream decision.

If any step in that chain breaks alignment, it's not UbD anymore. That's the hardest engineering problem we solved — not generating content, but keeping everything connected."

## SECTION 4: THE VISION — Who This Serves (3:45 - 4:15)

**Say something like:**

"The Vibe the Gap challenge asks: what happens when the person closest to the problem can finally build the solution — without needing a developer?

For teachers, this is that moment. There are 3.7 million teachers in the United States. Every one of them plans curriculum. Most do it alone, with tools that haven't changed in 20 years. Backward Builder doesn't replace their expertise — it amplifies it. The teacher brings the domain knowledge. The AI handles the construction.

This is the tool I wished I'd had when I was teaching. And now it exists — for every teacher."

> Clean ending. Don't trail off. Pause, then stop recording.

## KEY PHRASES TO WEAVE IN

From the challenge docs — judges (AI and human) will recognize these:

- "The person closest to the problem" ← direct from Vibe the Gap challenge
- "No developer required" ← challenge thesis
- "Domain expertise" ← what the teacher brings
- "Alignment" ← what makes UbD work and BB different
- "Deploy" / "deployable" ← this is live software, not a document
- "Teacher as builder" ← the paradigm shift
- "3.7 million teachers" ← market size, say once for impact
- "Build for one Stacey really well" ← echoes challenge's advice

## WORDS TO AVOID

- "AI-powered" without showing what that means
- "We used the Claude API" without explaining HOW
- "This is like ChatGPT for teachers" (undersells)
- "We didn't have time to..." (reframe as roadmap/vision)
- Any jargon a non-teacher wouldn't understand (explain it)

## RECORDING TIPS

- **One take is fine.** Authenticity scores better than polish.
- **Type live.** Enter the enduring understanding by typing it. Judges notice pasting.
- **If something breaks:** "Let me try that again" — shows confidence and proves it's real.
- **Audio matters more than video.** Use a headset or sit close to a mic.
- **Add captions in CapCut.** Judges may skim on mute first.
- **File format:** MP4, under 100MB, under 5 minutes.
