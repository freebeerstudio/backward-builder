## SECTION 1: THE STORY (0:00 - 0:45)

> Voiceover while the landing page is on screen. Confident, personal.

"When I was a middle school teacher, I knew exactly what great teaching looked like. I'd been trained in Understanding by Design — start with what students need to understand, then design assessments backward from there, then build instruction that gets them ready.

But here's what they don't tell you in teacher prep: the tools you actually have are Word documents and blank templates. And that 45 minutes of planning time? It gets eaten by grading, emails, and parent calls before you even sit down.

I spent years wishing I could just describe what my students needed to understand and have something build the rest. I went on to earn my doctorate in education, work in edtech and administration, and eventually move into enterprise technology. And now, with AI, I finally built the tool I wished I'd had in that classroom.

This is Backward Builder."

---

## SECTION 2: THE DEMO — Creating a Unit Live (0:45 - 1:30)

> Click "Design a Unit" or the chat box. Type live — never paste.

"Let me show you what a teacher's experience looks like. I'm going to create a brand new unit right now — in real time."

> Set up classroom context if needed (7th grade, Science, Missouri).

"I start where UbD says to start — with the enduring understanding. What do I want my students to walk away truly understanding?"

> Type live: "Students will understand that organisms in an ecosystem are interdependent, and changes to one population affect the entire system."

"That's all I need to provide — my expertise as a teacher. What my students need to understand. The AI handles the construction."

> Watch the AI process. While it's working:

"The AI is now doing what would take me 20-30 minutes manually — mapping this to my state standards, classifying the cognitive level using Bloom's Taxonomy, and generating essential questions."

> When Stage 1 results appear, briefly show standards and Bloom's classification.

"It identified this as an 'Analyze' level understanding and mapped it to Missouri's life science standards. Stage 1 is done."

---

## SECTION 3: THE DEMO — Evidence & Learning Plan (1:30 - 2:30)

> Navigate to Stage 2.

"Stage 2 — the evidence. What would prove a student actually understands ecosystem interdependence?"

> Generate performance tasks. Show the GRASPS task and rubric.

"The AI generates a performance task using the GRASPS framework — Goal, Role, Audience, Situation, Product, Standards — with a detailed rubric. It also creates auto-graded Checks for Understanding aligned to the rubric criteria."

> Briefly show a check with its questions.

"These aren't generic quiz questions. Each one maps to a specific rubric criterion so I know exactly which skills to reteach."

> Navigate to Stage 3. Generate learning plan.

"Stage 3 — the learning plan. Every activity scaffolds toward the performance task. The AI sequences them so each lesson builds on the last."

> Show the activity timeline briefly.

"The whole unit — designed backward from the understanding, just like UbD demands. Now I click 'Complete Plan' and land on mission control."

---

## SECTION 4: MISSION CONTROL — The 5-Stage Overview (2:30 - 3:00)

> Click "Complete Plan" and land on the unit overview.

"This is mission control. I can see all five stages of my unit. Stages 1, 2, and 3 — the design — are complete. Stage 4, Go Live, is now unlocked. Stage 5, Results, is locked until I publish to students."

> Point out the locked Stage 5 with the padlock.

"This reflects how teachers actually work. I might design this unit on Sunday night but not go live until Tuesday. The unit is saved and ready whenever I am."

> Click "Go Live."

---

## SECTION 5: THE DEMO — Student Experience & Results (3:00 - 3:45)

> On the publish page, show the expandable cards with QR codes and share links.

"I go live, and now each check and performance task has a share link and QR code. I can project this in class or text the link. Students open it on any device — phone, Chromebook, tablet. No login. No app download."

> Open a student share link in a new tab or on your phone. Answer a couple questions.

"Here's what a student sees. Clean, simple — just the questions. They answer and submit."

> Go back to the results dashboard.

"And back on my side — real-time results. Per-question accuracy. I can see exactly where my class is struggling and what needs reteaching. No spreadsheets. No manual grading. Data I can act on before the next class."

## SECTION 6: SHARING & COMMUNITY (3:45 - 3:55)

> Go back to the unit overview. Show the Share & Collaborate section.

"And because great curriculum shouldn't live in one teacher's laptop — I can share this unit directly with a colleague by email, or publish it to the community library where any teacher can find it."

> Briefly show the community tab on the landing page.

---

## SECTION 7: TECHNICAL DEPTH (3:55 - 4:10)

> Voiceover, can show the unit overview or a code snippet.

"Under the hood, this isn't a single API call. It's a multi-step AI pipeline. The AI analyzes the understanding, classifies it with Bloom's Taxonomy, generates performance tasks calibrated to that level, creates checks aligned to the rubric criteria, and reverse-engineers learning activities. Each step feeds context to the next — and the enduring understanding anchors every downstream decision.

Maintaining that alignment chain is the hardest problem we solved."

---

## SECTION 8: THE CLOSE (4:10 - 4:25)

"The Vibe the Gap challenge asks: what happens when the person closest to the problem can finally build the solution?

There are 3.7 million teachers in the United States. Every one of them plans curriculum. Most do it alone, with tools that haven't changed in 20 years.

Backward Builder doesn't replace their expertise — it amplifies it. The teacher brings the domain knowledge. The AI handles the construction.

This is the tool I wished I'd had when I was teaching. And now it exists."

> Clean ending. Pause. Stop recording.

## RECORDING TIPS

- One clean take. Authenticity beats polish.
- Type the enduring understanding live. Judges notice pasting.
- If the AI takes a moment to generate, narrate while waiting — that's natural.
- If something breaks: "Let me try that again" — shows confidence.
- Audio matters more than video. Use a headset.
- Add captions in CapCut after recording.
- MP4, under 100MB, under 5 minutes.
