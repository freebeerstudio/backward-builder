## THE ONE-LINER

**Backward Builder lets teachers go from "here's what students need to understand" to a complete unit plan with a live, auto-graded quiz — in minutes, not hours, no developer required.**

## THE STORY (Your Credibility Arc)

You're not someone who read about this problem in a blog post. You lived it.

**Act 1 — The Teacher.**
You were a middle school teacher. You knew what good curriculum looked like. You understood backward design — start with the enduring understanding, design assessments, then build instruction. But the tools you had were Word docs and blank templates. You spent your planning period grading instead of designing.

**Act 2 — The System.**
You moved into edtech, then administration, then enterprise technology. You saw the gap from every level: teachers who knew exactly what they needed, and tech teams who built tools that never quite fit. The people closest to the problem were always the furthest from the solution.

**Act 3 — The Builder.**
Now you're an AI developer. And you built the tool you wished you'd had in the classroom. Backward Builder doesn't replace teacher expertise — it amplifies it. The teacher brings the domain knowledge. The AI handles the construction.

## THE PROBLEM (Impact & Relevance = 40% of Score)

### The Pain — Make It Visceral

- 3.7 million teachers in the US plan curriculum
- Average teacher has ~45 minutes of planning time per day
- That time is consumed by grading, emails, parent calls, IEP paperwork
- Curriculum planning gets squeezed into evenings, weekends, or not at all
- UbD (Understanding by Design) is the gold standard — but it's time-intensive to do well
- Teachers KNOW what great curriculum looks like. They don't have tools that match how they think.

### The Gap — Tie It to Challenge #2

Current tools either:
- **Do nothing** (blank Word docs, generic templates)
- **Do too much** (enterprise curriculum platforms that cost $50K+ and require district adoption)
- **Miss the point** (AI tools that generate "lesson plans" without understanding backward design, alignment, or pedagogy)

No tool lets a teacher start with an enduring understanding and end with a deployable, auto-graded assessment and aligned learning activities — the way UbD actually works.

### The "Telephone Game" Connection

The Vibe the Gap challenge describes software's "telephone game" — by the time an idea passes through layers of people, it barely resembles what was needed. Education has the same problem:

- Curriculum standards are set by state boards
- Interpreted by district curriculum coordinators  
- Translated into scope-and-sequence by instructional coaches
- Finally reach the classroom teacher, who has to figure out what to actually DO with it

Backward Builder skips the telephone game. The teacher — the person closest to the students — defines the understanding and builds directly from it.

## THE SOLUTION (Keep It Concrete)

### The Core Loop (This Is What You Demo)

1. **Teacher inputs an enduring understanding** — "Students will understand that..."
2. **AI generates aligned assessments** — calibrated to the cognitive level of the understanding
3. **Teacher selects/modifies assessments** — professional judgment in the loop
4. **Backward Builder deploys a live quiz** — shareable link, auto-graded, results dashboard
5. **AI generates sequenced learning activities** — aligned backward from the assessment

### What Makes It Different

- **Starts where UbD starts** — with understanding, not with activities or content
- **Teacher is the expert** — AI builds, teacher decides
- **Produces real, deployable tools** — not a document. A working quiz students can take right now.
- **Everything stays aligned** — the enduring understanding anchors every downstream decision

## HANDLING JUDGE QUESTIONS

**"Why teachers? Why not a more general tool?"**
> "The challenge says to niche down — and I agree. A vibe-coding tool for everyone is a tool for no one. By going deep on teachers and UbD specifically, the AI can speak their language, anticipate their workflow, and produce something that feels like magic instead of a generic chatbot. Build for one Stacey really well before you try to build for all of them."

**"How is this different from ChatGPT?"**
> "ChatGPT can generate a lesson plan if you prompt it well enough. But it doesn't understand UbD as a framework. It doesn't maintain alignment across stages. It doesn't deploy a live quiz. And it definitely doesn't give you an auto-graded results dashboard. This is a structured tool, not a chatbot."

**"Could this scale beyond teachers?"**
> "The pattern absolutely scales. 'Domain expert inputs what they know, AI builds the digital tool' works for any field. But the insight from the challenge is that the narrower you go, the better the experience. Teachers and UbD is where I started because it's a domain I know deeply — and it proves the concept for everywhere else."

**"What would you build next?"**
> "Three things: (1) Standards alignment — automatically map enduring understandings to state standards so teachers get compliance for free. (2) Student progress tracking across multiple units, not just one quiz. (3) A sharing layer — teachers share their best units with each other and the library gets smarter over time."
